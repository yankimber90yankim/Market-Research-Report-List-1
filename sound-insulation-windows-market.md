<p><p>The sound insulation windows market primarily features two types: laminated glass and vacuum glass. Laminated glass consists of multiple layers of glass bonded with interlayers, effectively reducing noise transmission while enhancing safety. Vacuum glass, on the other hand, utilizes an airless space between two glass panes to minimize sound and improve thermal insulation. Both types are designed to create quieter indoor environments, making them popular in residential and commercial buildings, particularly in urban areas with high noise levels.</p></p>
