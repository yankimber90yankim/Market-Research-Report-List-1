<p><p>Manual welders encompass various types of welding techniques, including projection welding and spot welding. Projection welding involves the use of projections on the workpieces that concentrate heat in specific areas, allowing for efficient joining of metals. Spot welding, on the other hand, creates welds at discrete points by applying pressure and electrical current to the overlapping materials. Both methods are widely used in industries such as automotive and manufacturing, providing strength and durability to welded structures while optimizing productivity and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
