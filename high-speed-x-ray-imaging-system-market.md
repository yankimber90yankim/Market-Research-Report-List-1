<p><p>High-speed X-ray imaging systems are categorized into two main types based on frame rates: less than 30 frames per second (fps) and more than 30 fps. Systems with less than 30 fps are typically used for applications requiring lower temporal resolution, such as static inspections or quality control in manufacturing. In contrast, systems exceeding 30 fps are designed for capturing dynamic events, making them suitable for medical diagnostics, real-time monitoring, and research applications where motion detail is critical.</p></p>
