<p><p>The electronics plastic market is divided into two primary types: halogenated and non-halogenated. Halogenated plastics contain halogen elements, which enhance flame retardancy but raise environmental and health concerns during disposal and recycling. Non-halogenated plastics, on the other hand, are designed without halogen compounds, making them more environmentally friendly and safer while still offering adequate flame resistance. The choice between these types often depends on regulatory requirements, performance characteristics, and end-user preferences in various electronic applications.</p></p>
