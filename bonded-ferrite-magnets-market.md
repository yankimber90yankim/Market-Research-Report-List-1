<p><p>Bonded ferrite magnets are composite materials made by mixing ferrite powder with a polymer binder, offering cost-effectiveness and versatility. Injected magnets are produced by injecting the ferrite-polymer mixture into molds, allowing for complex shapes and high precision, ideal for small, intricate components. Pressed magnets are formed by compressing the mixture under high pressure, resulting in higher magnetic strength and density, suitable for larger, robust applications. Both types cater to diverse industries, balancing performance, cost, and design flexibility.</p></p>
