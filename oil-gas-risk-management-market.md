<p><p>In the oil and gas sector, Initial Risk Management focuses on identifying and mitigating potential hazards during the exploration and production phases. This proactive approach aims to minimize risks before they impact operations. In contrast, Residual Risk Management addresses the risks that remain after initial measures are implemented. It involves ongoing monitoring, assessment, and strategies to manage these residual risks effectively. Together, these two market types ensure the safe and efficient operation of oil and gas activities, enhancing sustainability and profitability.</p></p>
