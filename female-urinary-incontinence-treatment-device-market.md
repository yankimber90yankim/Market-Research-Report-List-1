<p><p>The female urinary incontinence treatment device market primarily features two types: urethral devices and internal vaginal devices. Urethral devices focus on obstructing urine flow to prevent leakage, often utilizing mechanisms like plugs or catheters. Internal vaginal devices, on the other hand, support pelvic floor muscles and promote bladder control, often comprising intravaginal supports or inflatable balloons. Both types aim to enhance quality of life by managing incontinence symptoms and providing women with effective, non-invasive treatment options.</p></p>
