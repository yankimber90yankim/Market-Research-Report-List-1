<p><p>The aircraft and aerospace die casting components market is primarily categorized into hot die casting and cold die casting. Hot die casting involves pouring molten metal into heated molds, enabling better fluidity and complex shapes, making it suitable for high-temperature applications. In contrast, cold die casting utilizes lower temperature processes, allowing for precise dimensional control and surface finish. Both methods are essential for producing lightweight, durable components that meet stringent aerospace standards, ensuring performance and safety in aviation applications.</p></p>
