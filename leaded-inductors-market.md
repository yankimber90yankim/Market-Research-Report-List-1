<p><p>Leaded inductors are essential components in electronic circuits, available in various market types including axial, radial, and through-hole configurations. Axial inductors have leads extending from both ends, facilitating easy placement in tight spaces. Radial inductors feature leads that extend from the bottom, offering compact designs ideal for printed circuit boards. Through-hole inductors are inserted into pre-drilled holes in a PCB, ensuring secure connections. Each type caters to different applications, balancing factors such as size, performance, and mounting preferences.</p></p>
