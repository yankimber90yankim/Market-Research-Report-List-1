<p><p>The pass transistor switching-mode power supply (SMPS) market is divided into two primary types: voltage mode control and current mode control. Voltage mode control regulates output voltage by adjusting the duty cycle of the switching transistor, ensuring stable performance under varying load conditions. Current mode control, on the other hand, adds an additional layer by limiting peak inductor current, enhancing efficiency and response times. Both methods offer distinct advantages, catering to different application needs in power management solutions.</p></p>
